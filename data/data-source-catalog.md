# data source catalog

This is a working index for social data sources. Its purpose is to help readers find data maintained by their original publishers and to record whether those data are suitable for social analysis. It is not intended to reproduce every dataset in this repository.

Entries marked **historical** have not yet been reviewed for current availability, definitions, licensing, or access methods.

## information to record for each source

- **Publisher and canonical location:** who maintains the data and where the authoritative version lives.
- **Geographic coverage and unit of analysis:** countries, regions, communities, households, individuals, facilities, or other units.
- **Thematic coverage:** the social conditions, risks, services, institutions, or outcomes represented.
- **Time coverage and update frequency:** whether the source supports current, trend, or longitudinal analysis.
- **Definitions and comparability:** whether indicators and methods remain consistent across places and time.
- **Disaggregation:** available breakdowns by sex, age, disability, identity, location, income, or other relevant characteristics.
- **Access:** downloadable formats, APIs, documentation, authentication requirements, and whether access is programmatic.
- **Maintenance and reliability:** evidence that the source is updated, documented, and preserved.
- **License and use constraints:** terms governing reuse, redistribution, and publication.
- **Repository or site dependency:** whether a local file currently supports a page, chart, notebook, or analysis and must be retained.
- **Review date and notes:** when the entry was last checked and any known limitations.

## status labels

- **candidate:** identified for later review.
- **active:** reviewed and currently useful.
- **site dependency:** currently supports material on this site and should not be removed without a replacement.
- **historical:** retained to document earlier work but not maintained as current data.
- **historical index:** an older list that may contain useful leads but requires source-by-source review.
- **retired reference:** no longer recommended for analysis, but retained as documentation.
